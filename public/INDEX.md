## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Don't change everything at once**
   - Update company name first
   - Then features
   - Then testimonials
   - Then design
   - Then measure

2. **Use the copy as-written first**
   - It's been thought through
   - Launch it
   - Then A/B test variations
   - Change what doesn't work

3. **Track everything from day one**
   - Every button click
   - Every form submission
   - Every section scroll
   - Use this to optimize later

4. **Keep the problem/solution structure**
   - It's proven
   - The order matters
   - Don't rearrange sections

5. **Update testimonials quarterly**
   - Refresh with recent customers
   - Rotate out older quotes
   - Keep them specific and authentic

6. **Your FAQ is your roadmap**
   - What questions appear there?
   - Those are your top concerns
   - Address them in the features/pricing

7. **Mobile first**
   - Test on real phones
   - Not just browser resize
   - Ask users to test

8. **Keep it honest**
   - No false claims
   - Real numbers only
   - Real testimonials
   - It builds trust

## ❓ Common Questions

**Q: Can I use this for a different product?**
A: Yes. Update features, testimonials, pricing, and FAQ. Keep structure.

**Q: Can I change the tone/voice?**
A: Read COPYWRITING_GUIDELINES.md first. Change gradually, test impact.

**Q: How long should I wait before A/B testing?**
A: 2-4 weeks of data. Then test one thing at a time.

**Q: Should I include video?**
A: Yes. Add demo video to hero section (reference in IMPLEMENTATION_CHEATSHEET.md).

**Q: What if our pricing is different?**
A: Update numbers in pricing section. Keep the 3-tier structure (proven).

**Q: How do I know if it's working?**
A: Track metrics from IMPLEMENTATION_CHEATSHEET.md. Compare to benchmarks in README.md.

**Q: Can I add more sections?**
A: Yes, but put them in the right place (after features, before pricing is usually best).

**Q: How often should I update?**
A: Monthly (count, testimonials), quarterly (features), 6 months (full refresh).
